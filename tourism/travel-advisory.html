Travel Advisory

Permit requirements, road conditions, best travel time, and safety tips — all in one place before you go.

✅ Currently Open: Hunza Valley is accessible via KKH (Karakoram Highway). Road conditions are good. No special permit required for Pakistani nationals.
Road Open (KKH operative)
No Permit Required (Pakistani nationals)
Foreign Nationals (NOC required)
📋

Permit Requirements

Pakistani nationals — No permit needed for Hunza main valley
For Khunjerab Pass — CNIC required at checkpost
Foreign nationals — NOC from Ministry of Interior required
Permit obtained from DC office Gilgit or online via NADRA portal
🗓

Best Time to Visit

April–June: Cherry blossom season — most beautiful, mild weather
July–September: Peak tourist season — all roads open, warm days
October–November: Autumn colors — fewer tourists, cooler
December–March: Heavy snowfall — some roads may close
🚗

Road & Travel Info

Distance from Islamabad: ~600 km via KKH
Travel time: 14–16 hours by road (coaster/jeep)
Flights available: Islamabad → Gilgit (45 min, weather dependent)
Road condition: KKH paved but narrow — drive carefully at night
Fuel stations: Available in Gilgit — fill up before heading to Hunza
⚠️

Safety Tips

Carry CNIC — checkposts on KKH will verify identity
Mobile signal: Telenor and Jazz available in Hunza town
Altitude sickness possible — rest on first day, drink water
Carry cash — limited ATMs available in Karimabad
Emergency contact: Gilgit Rescue: 1122
⚠️ Seasonal Advisory: Skardu flights are weather-dependent and frequently cancelled. Book road transport as backup. High altitude — acclimatization recommended.
Road Open (via Gilgit)
Flights Uncertain (weather dependent)
No Permit (Pakistani nationals)
📋

Permit Requirements

Pakistani nationals — No permit for Skardu city and Shangrila
Deosai National Park — Entry fee + vehicle registration required
K2 base camp — Separate permit from Alpine Club of Pakistan
Foreign nationals — NOC mandatory for entire GB region
🗓

Best Time to Visit

May–September: Best months — all roads including Deosai open
June–July: Deosai plains fully green — wildflowers blooming
October: Cold but beautiful — less crowded
Nov–April: Deosai closed due to snow — avoid unless experienced
🚗

Road & Travel Info

Distance from Islamabad: ~700 km via KKH + Gilgit
By road: 20–22 hours — overnight journey recommended
By flight: 1 hour from Islamabad — book early, frequent cancellations
4x4 jeep required for Deosai — cannot go in normal car
⚠️

Safety Tips

Altitude: Skardu is at 2,228m — give yourself 1 day to adjust
Deosai is at 4,114m — serious altitude sickness risk, go slow
Bears in Deosai — do not leave camp unattended at night
Carry warm clothes even in summer — nights are freezing
Emergency: Skardu CMH: 05811-920333
ℹ️ Peak Season Notice: July–August is extremely busy. Book hotels in advance. Saif-ul-Malook can only be reached by jeep or horse — no private cars allowed beyond Naran.
Road Open (Mansehra to Naran)
No Permit Required
Jeep Only (beyond Naran)
📋

Permit Requirements

No permit required for Pakistani nationals
Saif-ul-Malook — small entry fee at lakeside
Babusar Top — checkpost, CNIC verification
Lulusar Lake — no permit, free access
🗓

Best Time to Visit

June–August: Road fully open — peak tourist season
September: Quieter, cooler — great for photography
May: Snow still on peaks — dramatic views, some roads closed
Oct–May: Road closed beyond Balakot — Naran inaccessible
🚗

Road & Travel Info

Distance from Islamabad: ~250 km via Mansehra
Travel time: 6–7 hours by car or coaster
Jeep mandatory for Saif-ul-Malook, Lulusar, Babusar
Jeep rates: ~PKR 2,500–4,000 from Naran jeep stand
⚠️

Safety Tips

Flash floods possible July–August — don't camp near rivers
Saif-ul-Malook: go early morning — afternoon clouds block views
Landslide prone area — check NDMA alerts before travel
Mobile signal: Jazz/Telenor in Naran town, none beyond
Emergency: Rescue 1122 Mansehra
⚠️ Difficult Access: Road to Raikot Bridge is extremely dangerous — world's one of most dangerous roads. Only experienced jeep drivers allowed. Not recommended for families with children.
Difficult Road (jeep only)
No Permit Required
Open May–Oct
📋

Permit Requirements

No permit required for Pakistani nationals
Register at Raikot Bridge checkpost — CNIC required
Foreign nationals — NOC from GB government required
Camping equipment must be declared at checkpost
🗓

Best Time to Visit

June–August: Best time — meadows lush green, Nanga Parbat clear
May: Snow still present — dramatic but cold camping
September: Fewer tourists, still accessible
Oct–May: Closed — heavy snow makes trail impossible
🚗

Road & Travel Info

From Islamabad: Drive to Raikot Bridge (~8 hrs) via KKH
Raikot Bridge to Tato village: Jeep ride (~45 min, very steep)
Tato to Fairy Meadows: 3–4 hour hike on foot
No cars beyond Raikot Bridge — jeep only, Rs 2000–3000
⚠️

Safety Tips

Jeep road is one of world's most dangerous — don't look down
Hire only local experienced jeep drivers from Raikot
No mobile signal — inform family before going
Carry all food, water, medicine — nothing available at meadows
Temperature drops to 0°C at night even in summer
✅ Good Conditions: Neelum Valley road is open and accessible. No special permits required for Pakistani nationals. Arang Kel cable car is operational.
Road Open
No Permit Required
Cable Car Operational
📋

Permit Requirements

No permit for Pakistani nationals in main Neelum Valley
CNIC required at AJK entry checkpost near Muzaffarabad
Kel and Arang Kel — small conservation fee applies
Foreign nationals — Special permit from AJK government required
🗓

Best Time to Visit

May–September: Best — road fully open, greenery at peak
March–April: Spring blossoms — less crowded
October: Autumn colors — stunning photography
Dec–Feb: Some sections snow-blocked — road closures possible
🚗

Road & Travel Info

Distance from Islamabad: ~160 km via Muzaffarabad
Travel time: 4–5 hours to Muzaffarabad, 2 more hours to Keran
Arang Kel: Boat across river + cable car (or 3 hour hike)
Road is narrow and winding — drive carefully, avoid night driving
⚠️

Safety Tips

Sensitive border area — stay on main tourist routes
Mobile signal: good in Muzaffarabad, weak beyond Keran
River crossings — only use authorized boats
Carry cash — no ATMs beyond Muzaffarabad
Emergency: AJK Rescue: 1122
✅ Fully Safe & Open: Swat Valley is completely safe for tourists. No permits required. Malam Jabba ski resort and chairlift are operational. Recommended for families.
Road Open
No Permit Required
Family Friendly
📋

Permit Requirements

No permit required for Pakistani nationals
Malam Jabba ski resort — ticket required at gate
Chairlift — separate ticket, approx PKR 500/person
Foreign nationals — register at hotel, no special permit needed
🗓

Best Time to Visit

April–June: Lush green, cool weather — best for sightseeing
December–February: Snow season — skiing at Malam Jabba
July–September: Busy season — book hotels in advance
October: Autumn — colorful forests, pleasant temperature
🚗

Road & Travel Info

Distance from Islamabad: ~250 km via Motorway + Swat Expressway
Travel time: 4–5 hours by car or coaster
Swat Expressway: excellent condition — fast and safe drive
Malam Jabba: 40 km from Saidu Sharif — winding mountain road
⚠️

Safety Tips

Swat is completely safe — active tourism police presence
Mobile signal: excellent throughout Swat valley
ATMs available in Mingora — carry cash for mountain areas
Buddhist ruins at Butkara — respectful behavior required
Emergency: Rescue 1122 Swat
